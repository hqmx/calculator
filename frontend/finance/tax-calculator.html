Understanding Tax Calculations

Whether you're calculating Sales Tax, VAT (Value Added Tax), or GST (Goods and Services Tax), the math is generally the same.

Formulas:

Add Tax: Total = Net Amount Ã— (1 + Rate/100)

Remove Tax: Net Amount = Total / (1 + Rate/100)

Tax Amount: Total - Net Amount

Common Tax Rates:

  • VAT (UK/EU): Typically 20% (Standard)
  • GST (Australia): 10%
  • Sales Tax (US): Varies by state (0% - 10%+)
  • Consumption Tax (Japan): 10%

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Sales Tax and VAT?

Sales Tax is collected only at the final sale to the consumer. VAT is collected at every stage of production and distribution. However, for the final consumer, the calculation method is often the same (percentage added to price).

Yes! GST (Goods and Services Tax) works exactly like VAT. Just enter your local GST rate (e.g., 10% for Australia, 5% for Canada) to calculate.
