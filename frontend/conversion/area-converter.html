About Area Conversion

Area is a measure of the size of a two-dimensional surface. It's commonly used in real estate, construction, agriculture, and land surveying. Different countries and industries use different units of area measurement.

Metric Units

  • Square Millimeter (mm²): Very small areas, technical drawings
  • Square Centimeter (cm²): Small objects, packaging
  • Square Meter (m²): Floor area, room size, small land plots
  • Hectare (ha): Agricultural land, parks (1 ha = 10,000 m²)
  • Square Kilometer (km²): Cities, countries, large territories

Imperial Units

  • Square Inch (in²): Small surfaces, technical specifications
  • Square Foot (ft²): Room size, house area (most common in US real estate)
  • Square Yard (yd²): Carpeting, fabric, medium-sized areas
  • Acre (ac): Land plots, farms (1 acre = 43,560 ft²)
  • Square Mile (mi²): Large territories, geographic regions

Frequently Asked Questions

How many square feet are in an acre?

One acre equals 43,560 square feet. This is a standard land measurement in the United States.

What's the difference between a hectare and an acre?

A hectare is a metric unit (10,000 m²) while an acre is an imperial unit (4,047 m²). One hectare equals approximately 2.47 acres.

How do I calculate the area of a rectangle?

Multiply length by width: Area = L × W. Make sure both measurements are in the same unit before multiplying.

What's the standard apartment size?

It varies by location, but typically ranges from 30-100 m² (320-1,075 ft²) for 1-3 bedroom apartments.

Why do area units square when converting?

Because area is two-dimensional, you must square the conversion factor. For example: 1 m = 3.28 ft, so 1 m² = (3.28)² = 10.76 ft².
