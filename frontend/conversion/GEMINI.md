### Description
Convert temperatures between Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F), and Kelvin (K). Fast, accurate, and easy to use for weather, cooking, science, and travel.

### Supported Units
- **Celsius (°C)**: Metric standard, water freezes at 0°C
- **Fahrenheit (°F)**: US standard, water freezes at 32°F
- **Kelvin (K)**: Scientific absolute scale, absolute zero is 0 K

### Features
1. **Three Temperature Scales**
   - Celsius (metric)
   - Fahrenheit (imperial)
   - Kelvin (absolute)

2. **Instant Conversion**
   - Real-time calculation
   - Precise formulas
   - Reference points displayed

3. **Practical Use**
   - Weather forecasts
   - Cooking temperatures
   - Scientific research
   - International travel

### How to Use (3 Steps)
1. **Select Source Scale**: Choose Celsius, Fahrenheit, or Kelvin
2. **Enter Temperature**: Type the temperature value
3. **View Results**: See conversions to other scales instantly

### Examples
1. **Example 1: Weather**
   - From: 25°C
   - To Fahrenheit: 77°F
   - To Kelvin: 298.15 K

2. **Example 2: Cooking**
   - From: 350°F
   - To Celsius: 176.67°C
   - To Kelvin: 449.82 K

3. **Example 3: Science**
   - From: 273.15 K
   - To Celsius: 0°C
   - To Fahrenheit: 32°F

### Conversion Formulas

**Celsius to Fahrenheit**:
```
°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
```

**Fahrenheit to Celsius**:
```
°C = (°F - 32) × 5/9
```

**Celsius to Kelvin**:
```
K = °C + 273.15
```

**Kelvin to Celsius**:
```
°C = K - 273.15
```

**Fahrenheit to Kelvin**:
```
K = (°F - 32) × 5/9 + 273.15
```

**Kelvin to Fahrenheit**:
```
°F = (K - 273.15) × 9/5 + 32
```

### Temperature Reference Points

| Event | Celsius | Fahrenheit | Kelvin |
|-------|---------|------------|--------|
| Absolute Zero | -273.15°C | -459.67°F | 0 K |
| Water Freezes | 0°C | 32°F | 273.15 K |
| Room Temperature | 20-22°C | 68-72°F | 293-295 K |
| Body Temperature | 37°C | 98.6°F | 310.15 K |
| Water Boils | 100°C | 212°F | 373.15 K |

### FAQ
**Q: Which countries use Fahrenheit?**
A: Primarily the United States, Bahamas, Belize, and Cayman Islands. Most other countries use Celsius.

**Q: What is Kelvin used for?**
A: Kelvin is used in scientific contexts, especially physics and chemistry. It starts at absolute zero (no negative values).

**Q: Why is 0°C equal to 32°F?**
A: These are different scales. Fahrenheit sets water's freezing point at 32° and boiling at 212° (180-degree range).

**Q: Can temperature be negative in Kelvin?**
A: No. Kelvin starts at absolute zero (0 K), the lowest possible temperature. Negative Kelvin doesn't exist.
